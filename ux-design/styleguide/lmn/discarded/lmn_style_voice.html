tone of voice

lastminute.com — play with your words

Pink isn’t just a colour. It’s a philosophy. It’s a state of mind. It’s a tone of voice.

We’re known for our wit and our clever lines. We’re inspiring and fun. And we can get away with a bit more than other brands.

So we need to find smart, amusing and brave ways to say what we have to say. It means being fearless with our words. Playing around, breaking the norm and surprising people.

How to play with your words

Sharpen up
Keep it short and sharp. One witty headline is worth a thousand puns. But sharp headlines aren’t easy and won’t always be the first thing that pops into your head. So take your time, try out a few options and test it out on the person next to you.

No puns please
Don’t use lazy puns. Read your words out to someone else. And if they prompt a groan, cut them out.

Cut the smut
If it’s a cliché, if it’s too wink wink, nudge nudge, leave it out. We want to make people smile, not roll their eyes.

Do the twist
Surprise your girlfriend, take her sister to Paris is a famous lastminute.com line from days of old. It works because it takes the expected and turns it on its head. It’s not obvious, so it surprises you (and the girlfriend).

Ha ha ha. Stop
If you make a joke, don’t push it. It’s tiring and dilutes the wit. Be confident your readers will find something funny and stop there.

Start small
Focus on a little-known fact, an intriguing place to visit or a bizarre hotel and work your way out from there. It’ll help you avoid generic travel clichés, too.

For example, every year galleries in Paris stay open all night – for one night only. Start with this fact to get people’s attention, and then talk about the city itself.

Not too close
We’re matey, but not over familiar or in your face. Be friendly and conversational, but don’t bound in and sit on a customer’s lap. Keep up the contractions (we’ll, you’ll) to sound chatty, and use questions to draw people in.

Lively links
Obviously links need to help people find exactly what they’re after. But often they’re plain and descriptive. So spice them up a bit. Play with them - weave them into a story or write them in an unusual way. But make sure they’re clear, and check with the search engineers too, so people can always find what they want. For example, write put on your technicolour dreamcoat as a link to Joseph tickets.

Break the rules
If it sounds good, do it differently. That’s what we’re all about.

Play with your words — the do’s and the dont's

Do, do, do

  • Do use sharp, witty headlines
  • Do twist your words and turn things on their head
  • Do start with a small detail and build out from there
  • Do play with your links (but keep them clear)
  • Do break the rules (but only if you come up with something brilliant)

Don’t, don’t, don’t

  • Don’t use lazy puns
  • Don’t be smutty
  • Don’t use travel clichés or sales speak
  • Don’t be hyperactive or forced
  • Don’t push the jokes
