Tone of voice

Travelocity – give them your word

We’re Can-do TravelAngels. We watch over you when you’re going away and make the new familiar. With us, there are no missed planes or lost tickets. No hiccups. No crossed-wires. We work behind the scenes to make sure everything runs smoothly.

And like a friendly guide, we’re with you wherever you roam. We give you useful support and tips. And we tell you the good and the bad, so you know what’s what. Everyone should pack a TravelAngel.

Can-do TravelAngels only speak when you need them to. We’re exact. So speak up when it’s most useful, but be personal, too. Don’t just say it, reassure people. Give them your word. Here’s how.

How to give them your word

Talk to people
If you were on the phone, what would you say? Make your writing conversational. So don’t slip into sales speak. Read your words aloud, and if they sound unnatural, cut them.

A word in your ear
Imagine you’re speaking to one person, even though you’re writing for many. Use personal pronouns (you and us) and think about what your reader really wants to know. Then tell them.

Get to the point
Ditch the adjectives that tell the reader how fantastic or amazing something is. Get straight to the point and show people you know what you’re talking about by giving them real examples.

Are you really helping?
Don’t write something for the sake of it. If it’s a good tip, write it. If it’s not useful, leave it out.
